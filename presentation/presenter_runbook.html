Per-person scripts

J
Jeenal
Opener · slides 1–3
~1.5 min
  • Introduce the team and topic in one line.
  • "A GAN is two networks: a generator that makes fakes from noise, and a discriminator that judges real vs fake. They compete — the generator tries to fool the discriminator."
  • "GANs power face generation, image-to-image, super-resolution, and data augmentation. Every one needs variety — hold onto that word."
Hand-off: "But sometimes the generator stops making variety. Rohit?"
R
Rohit
Core + demo driver · slides 4–6
~2 min
  • "Mode collapse = the generator loses variety. A 'mode' is one type — say ten kinds of animal. A healthy generator makes all ten; a collapsed one makes one. Like a student who only ever draws a cat because the examiner keeps accepting it."
  • On the mechanism visual: "Gray bars are the real modes, violet is what the generator makes. Left, it covers all. Right, it over-pumps one and abandons the rest — it finds the easiest mode that fools the judge, the judge adapts, and it hops to another single mode instead of spreading."
  • On the faces slide: "Concretely — on our own faces. Ask for any of us, the collapsed model returns Sharvan every time."
Hand-off: "So why does it get stuck? Sharvan."
S
Sharvan
Why it happens · slide 7
~1.25 min
  • "Three reasons it sticks: nothing in the basic loss rewards variety; the discriminator judges one sample at a time, so it can't punish 'these are all identical'; and the gradients don't push it to spread out."
  • "Two flavours — complete collapse is one output; partial collapse is a few types while missing many."
Hand-off: "How do we actually catch it? Disha."
D
Disha
Detect + runs the live demo · slide 8
~2 min
  • "We catch it by checking diversity: eyeball the samples, count the classes, walk the noise, and watch the metrics."
  • "Let's watch it live."
Demo cue → Rohit: press space (Collapse run). Narrate: "Early on, the animals are varied — coverage near 10/10. As we train, the cells turn orange and every one becomes a cat — coverage falls toward 1/10. Same output for very different inputs — that's collapse."
Hand-off: "So how do we fix it? Pujan."
P
Pujan
Fix + close + leads Q&A · slides 9–10
~2 min
  • "The fixes force variety back: minibatch discrimination lets the discriminator see a whole batch and punish sameness; WGAN gives better gradients; PacGAN packs samples; feature matching matches real-data statistics."
  • "Watch what a fix does."
Demo cue → Rohit: press F (Reveal the fix). Narrate: "The animals diversify again and coverage climbs back to 10/10."
  • Takeaway: "Mode collapse = the generator trades variety for an easy win. Catch it by measuring diversity, fix it by forcing full coverage. It's the GAN version of a poisoned response — one output regardless of input."
  • "Happy to take questions."
