Per-person scripts

J
Jeenal
Sets up the game · hook + GAN recap + Nash equilibrium
~1.5 min
  • Introduce the team and topic in one line.
  • "A GAN is two networks: a generator that makes fakes from noise, and a discriminator that judges real vs fake. They train by competing — the generator tries to fool the discriminator."
  • "It's a two-player game, so it has a Nash equilibrium: the point where neither network can improve by changing alone. There it means the generator's fakes perfectly match the real data and the discriminator is reduced to guessing — 50/50. That balance is what training is trying to reach."
Hand-off: "But the game can break before it reaches that balance. Rohit?"
R
Rohit
Shows what breaks · definition + mechanism + complete vs partial · demo driver
~2 min
  • "Mode collapse = the generator loses variety. A 'mode' is one type — say ten kinds of animal. A healthy generator makes all ten; a collapsed one makes one. Like a student who only ever draws a cat because the examiner keeps accepting it."
  • On the mechanism visual: "Gray bars are the real modes, violet is what the generator makes. It finds the easiest mode that fools the judge, the judge adapts, and it hops to another single mode instead of spreading."
  • "Two flavours: complete collapse is basically one output; partial collapse is a few types while missing many."
  • "On our own faces: ask for any of us and the collapsed model returns the same person every time."
Demo cue → Rohit: press space (Collapse run) and let it run to the end — the animals all become a cat, coverage falls to 1/10. Leave it collapsed on screen.
Hand-off: "Why does it break like this? It's in the loss. Sharvan."
S
Sharvan
Loss math · why JSD fails → WGAN loss fix
~2 min
  • "The original GAN minimises the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the fake and real distributions. The problem: when those two barely overlap — which is exactly what happens at collapse — JSD is almost constant, so its gradient is nearly zero. The generator gets no signal telling it to spread out."
  • "The fix is a better distance. WGAN uses the Wasserstein / Earth Mover's distance — the minimum cost of moving probability mass to turn one distribution into the other. It varies smoothly even when the distributions don't overlap, so there's a usable gradient everywhere, pushing the generator to cover all the modes."
  • "Practically: the discriminator becomes a 'critic' (no sigmoid) kept 1-Lipschitz by weight clipping, or a gradient penalty in WGAN-GP."
Demo cue → Rohit: press 1 (WGAN · loss fix). The animals diversify again, coverage climbs back to 10/10, and the explainer shows the technique. "That's the loss fix in action."
Hand-off: "Before we trust any fix, how do we even detect collapse? Disha."
D
Disha
Diagnosis · deceptive loss vs quantitative metrics
~1.5 min
  • "First, what doesn't work: the training loss is deceptive. GAN losses oscillate, and a collapsed generator can still show a perfectly reasonable-looking loss — so a healthy loss curve can hide total collapse."
  • "What does work is quantitative metrics. Inception Score (IS) — higher is better — rewards images that are both sharp and diverse. Fréchet Inception Distance (FID) — lower is better — compares fakes to real data and catches collapse because diversity drops. Plus simple mode coverage."
Demo cue → Rohit: press R (Reset), then drag the epoch slider to ~110 (collapsed). Disha points at the panel: "Look — the loss just wobbles and looks fine, but coverage is 1/10 and FID would be high. That's how we actually diagnose it."
Hand-off: "The loss fix was one route. There are structural fixes too. Pujan."
P
Pujan
Structural fixes + summary · leads Q&A
~2 min
  • "Instead of changing the loss, we can change the architecture or training. Minibatch discrimination lets the discriminator compare samples across a whole batch, so it can spot 'these are all identical' and penalise it — sameness becomes punishable."
  • "Unrolled GANs train the generator against a few look-ahead steps of the discriminator, so it can't cheaply exploit the current discriminator by collapsing onto one mode — it anticipates the response."
Demo cue → Rohit: press 2 (Minibatch discrimination). Coverage recovers and the explainer shows the technique. "A structural fix gets us the same recovery without touching the loss."
  • Summary: "Mode collapse = the generator trades variety for an easy win. We diagnose it with metrics like FID, not the loss; and we fix it either at the loss (WGAN) or structurally (minibatch discrimination, unrolled GAN). It's the GAN version of a poisoned response — one output regardless of input."
  • "Happy to take questions."
